## Overview

This repository contains the data analysis code for the working paper *"Esclusione dal mercato del lavoro e controllo mafioso del territorio agricolo: evidenza dalle province italiane"* (Labor Market Exclusion and Mafia Control of Agricultural Land: Evidence from Italian Provinces).

The paper investigates the relationship between youth labor market exclusion and the concentration of agricultural land confiscated from criminal organizations across Italian provinces. Using data from the Italian National Agency for Confiscated Assets (ANBSC), ISTAT demographic statistics, and the BES (Equitable and Sustainable Wellbeing) indicators for 2020, we construct a province-level index of mafia territorial presence in the agricultural sector.

**Main finding:** The youth labor non-participation rate alone explains 56% of the variance in the distribution of confiscated agricultural land across 104 Italian provinces (β = 0.061, p < 0.001), while GDP per capita is not significant once this variable is controlled for. The result is robust to spatial models, alternative specifications, and temporal robustness checks.

## Analysis Pipeline

The notebook is organized into the following sections:

1. **Data loading and cleaning** — loading all datasets, fixing encoding issues, standardizing municipality names via ISTAT codes
2. **Indicator construction** — building the normalized index of confiscated agricultural land per 100,000 inhabitants at the provincial level
3. **Descriptive analysis** — distribution of the index, top provinces, provincial aggregation
4. **Baseline OLS regression** — log-transformed dependent variable, youth labor non-participation rate and GDP per capita as regressors
5. **Robustness checks**
   - HC3 robust standard errors
   - Exclusion of extreme outliers
   - Restriction to provinces with documented mafia presence
   - Temporal robustness with 2019 regressors
   - Spatial Lag and Spatial Error models (Moran's I = 0.406, p = 0.001)
   - Tobit model with Average Marginal Effects (AME = 0.487)
   - Regional fixed effects
   - Alternative dependent variable (all confiscated assets)
6. **Choropleth maps** — municipal-level maps for Campania, Sicily, and Calabria

## Key Results

| Model | β (labor non-participation) | R² | N |
|-------|----------------------------|-----|---|
| Baseline OLS | 0.061*** | 0.561 | 104 |
| HC3 robust SE | 0.061*** | 0.561 | 104 |
| No outliers | 0.057*** | 0.554 | 102 |
| 2019 regressors | 0.058*** | 0.547 | 103 |
| Spatial Lag ML | 0.026*** | 0.728 | 104 |
| Tobit (levels) | 0.949*** | — | 103 |
| Regional FE | 0.004 (n.s.) | 0.806 | 103 |
| All assets (alt. DV) | 0.032*** | 0.191 | 98 |

The regional fixed effects result suggests the relationship operates primarily *between* regions rather than *within* regions — an important identification limitation discussed in the paper.

## Municipal-Level Geography

Choropleth maps reveal distinct geographic patterns reflecting the different organizational structures of Italy's main criminal organizations:

- **Campania** — compact cluster in the Caserta area (Casalesi clan)
- **Sicily** — diffuse pattern across the centre-west (Cosa Nostra)
- **Calabria** — concentrated in the southern tip around Reggio Calabria ('Ndrangheta)
